August & September Newsletter

  • Serendipity
  • September 30, 2020

Learning Bee Scratch - General Education Outreach

Starting our 3rd session of COVID-19 Distance Learning Scratch course with Learning Bee Learning, our students and teachers have been having a blast. In addition to working with new groups on our original core curriculum, we have also been able to expose returning students to new concepts like Variables, MyBlocks (also known as functions), and sprite costume manipulation. Our classes have grown to 4 groups and 14 students. We cannot wait to work with our students through October and prepare them for a Halloween themed project!

Friends of Children with Special Needs - Special Education Outreach

Besides our spring session and summer camp that we have hosted with Friends of Children with Special Needs (FCSN) previously, our partnership with the FCSN is continuing throughout our School Year Session. Utilizing our large team of experienced and seasoned instructors, we have divided the students into Beginner, Intermediate, and Advanced groups. Our classes have been expanding to more game-based projects including Pong, Mazes, Golf, and Personalized Garageband to discuss different coding concepts and make the classes more enjoyable and engaging for the students.

Fremont Unified School District Special Education Program - Special Education Outreach

Starting in October, we’ll be teaching Scratch classes to special education students in the Fremont Unified School District. This will be a smaller group of students than we usually teach, but we’re looking forward to being able to share coding and computer science with this group of students!

Future Plans

We have also open-sourced our Scratch Studio for anyone to access, and are working on a curriculum page for our website to be able to share and showcase the curriculum we’ve built up over the last year. Additionally, we’ve been welcoming some new teachers to Serendipity from different teams around the Bay Area, and have been training them so they can begin teaching our students. If you’re interested in teaching or helping out with Serendipity, contact us!
